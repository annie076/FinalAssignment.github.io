The piano has gone through centuries of development which has lead us to the modern pianos we have today. Modern pianos have 88 keys the span through 7 octaves. In addition, there are around 230 strings, a higher note will have 3 strings per key and a lower note will have 1 to 2 strings per key. Strings will determine things like volume or resonance of a key. They are typically made with steel and are sometimes wrapped in copper wire, much stronger compared to the first piano. Modern pianos aren't restricted to one style either. There are many unique and creative pianos that range from different shapes to even animals. Newer pianos can also be electric, like the keyboard. Electric pianos are much cheaper compared to grand pianos and are a good option for those who are beginners.
